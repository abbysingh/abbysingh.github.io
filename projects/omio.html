Omio - Make travel easy.

I have been working at Omio since February 2018 as a Product Designer for the search and booking experience.

How do help travelers find the right trip?

One of the most important things in finding the right trip for our users is how the results are displayed when they perform a search on our product. When I joined the company we were in the process of user testing our search results page. At the same time there was a parallel initiative to make our website completely responsive. This meant taking the mobile design system and scaling it up to the desktop.

Here's what we originally had,



Our users found a few issues with the search results,

  • Is that a bus or a train?
  • Is that a one way price or a round trip price?
  • How can I see the full trip?


I started exploring different options based on this feedback from our users. I tried adding a label that explains the price, I improved the tap affordance for the ‘changes’ button. All of this while also figuring out a responsive systematic structure for the cell. Ranging from our smallest supported screen size of 320px all the way up to 1920px on desktop.



While coming up with these iterations, I realised that while scanning long lists of information, it is a lot easier if the information is structured in columns of similar information. In a few of the above explorations, you might see the departure and arrival time in the same column, I decided to not move ahead with that as it can be very confusing. After spending more time refining my ideas, I came up with the following designs.



This design was user tested via an HTML prototype that I developed with my colleague who also had a development background. A coded prototype helped us test more realistic interactions with live data. After testing this prototype, this design was rolled out to our users.

While designing the search results, we realised we need another way to show trip information. This is to help our users understand and review the selection they have made and hence reduce errors in booking the wrong date. Another issue that we were facing was that our users were unable to understand the difference between the outbound and return selection screen. I came up with an interaction which included a very particular UI choreography which helped users process the structure of information on the page and not be overloaded. You can see the interaction below.



This interaction gave us an 8% uplift in round trip bookings! Both of these design improvements together cumulatively gave us a 5% increase in bookings. We were successfully able to sell trips in a better way to our users.
